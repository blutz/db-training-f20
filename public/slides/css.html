Basic Selectors

  1. .your-class-name selects everything with
    class="your-class-name"
  2. #your-id-name selects the element with
    id="your-id-name"
  3. div selects the tag by name
  4. * selects everything

Chaining Selectors

  1. , means "or" (e.g. .class1, .class2)
  2. Running two selectors together (e.g. .class1.class2) means "and"
  3. Space means children and only selects the rightmost selector (e.g. .class1 .class2)

Specificity

What happens to styles that affect the same element?

Whichever selector is more specific wins. If they have the same specificity, whichever one is loaded last wins.

Specificity

  1. !important
  2. Inline styles
  3. # of IDs in selector
  4. # of classes (or other attributes) in selector
  5. # of tags in selector

Do this on procreate. div || div span || .myclass span || div.myclass.second p.myclass .container || #myid
